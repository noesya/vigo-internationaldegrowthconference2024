Heritage-based planning has remained a state-led activity in postcolonial India. The state used heritage planning as a tool to pursue unhindered economic growth and an exercise of nation-building. Dominant developmental actors focused on trickle-down economic theories based on unhindered growth to bring positive socio-economic transformations at the local heritage sites, which has largely failed to deliver inclusive growth. This session will re-imagine heritage-making in Bodh Gaya from a post-growth perspective.
