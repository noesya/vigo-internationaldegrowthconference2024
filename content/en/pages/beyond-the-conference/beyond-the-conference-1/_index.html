The organization of a scientific event of this nature represents a unique opportunity to bring civil society closer to science; to make known to citizens the work developed by researchers; to raise public awareness of the need to establish new ways of social development and to address the ecological transition; and to highlight the importance of society and scientific community to approach, understand each other and establish collaborations. With these objectives in mind, Pontevedra Degrowth 2024 comprises different activities focused on civil society, emphasizing young people, some of which will be developed over the year, and others that will be organized during the conference days.

As part of the conference, a series of initiatives will be held aimed at engaging the general population and encouraging active participation from attendees. These events will provide a unique opportunity for citizens to get involved in discussions related to the topics addressed during the conference and will include a variety of activities such as talks, exhibitions, interactive workshops, roundtable discussions, debate spaces, art performances, and more, offering a platform for the general population to engage and actively participate in the conference.

The schedule will be public, and anyone interested can attend for free these activities. To facilitate attendance, most of them will be conducted in the local languages, namely Galician and Spanish, and will take place in various venues scattered throughout the city, with the distance between them always being within a ten-minute walking radius. The spaces encompass Casa das Campás, the Provincial Museum of Pontevedra, and others.

Through these events, interaction, collaborative learning, and the dissemination of relevant information to address current and future challenges in ecological economics and degrowth are promoted.
