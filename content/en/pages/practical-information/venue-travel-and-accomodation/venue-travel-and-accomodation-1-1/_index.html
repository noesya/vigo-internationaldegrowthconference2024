Pontevedra is a city of over 80,000 inhabitants located in the northwest of the Iberian Peninsula, in the autonomous community of Galicia (Spain). Due to its organization system, with an urban model that is committed to pedestrianization of the streets, the city has become an international benchmark for urban quality and sustainability, receiving several awards over the last ten years, including the UN-Habitat Award, in 2014.

Pontevedra is located in the province of the same name, in an area known as the "Rías Baixas". This region of the autonomous community of Galicia is famous for its pleasant climate, its gastronomic offer, and its beautiful landscapes, both coastal and inland. These are some of the nearby places worth visiting: 

  • Combarro: is a small village a few kilometers from Pontevedra, famous for its large number of hórreos, typical old constructions in Galicia, generally made of stone and raised from the ground on several pillars, which were intended to store and preserve food away from moisture and animals. 
  • O Morrazo: is a peninsula located between the estuaries of Vigo and Pontevedra where you can find an infinite number of beaches, large and small, familiar and wild. In its westernmost area is Cabo Home, the closest land point to the Cíes Islands.
  • Vigo: this is the biggest town in the region, located only 15 minutes by train from the center of Pontevedra. June is a perfect time to visit this city when the streets are bustling with good weather but tourism is not yet excessive. If you go you should not miss a walk along the port and the old town, and a sunset from a bar on one of its sandy beaches, watching the sun go down behind the Cíes Islands.
  • Santiago de Compostela: although it is not part of the Rías Baixas, this is a must-see place as it is the capital of Galicia and one of the best-known cities in the autonomous community. It has been a World Heritage Site since 1985, and its cathedral is the destination point for the various pilgrimage routes of the Camino de Santiago (O Camiño, as it is known in Galicia).

These are just some of the them, but the offer is enormous. Those fortunate enough to extend their stay will have infinite options for all tastes: sea, inland, beach, mountains, cities, villages, culture, sports... If you are one of those lucky ones and have doubts, the members of the local organizing team will be happy to share their recommendations with you.
