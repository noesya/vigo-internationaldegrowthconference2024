Pontevedra is a city of over 80,000 inhabitants located in the northwest of the Iberian Peninsula, in the autonomous community of Galicia (Spain). The city is organised with an urban model committed to pedestrianization of the streets and has become an international benchmark for urban quality and sustainability, receiving several awards over the last ten years, including the UN-Habitat Award, in 2014, and hosting Placemaking Europe in 2022. 

Pontevedra is an ideal location to organize a conference on degrowth due to its progressive urban policies and commitment to sustainability. This Spanish city has gained international recognition for its successful implementation of pedestrian-friendly initiatives and promotion of alternative transportation methods. By prioritizing pedestrians and cyclists over cars, Pontevedra has transformed its urban landscape into a liveable, environmentally conscious space. 

The city's dedication to degrowth principles is evident in its emphasis on quality of life, social cohesion, and environmental sustainability. Pontevedra's car-free zones and well-connected public transportation system encourage delegates to experience a sustainable urban lifestyle firsthand. The city's compact size enables easy navigation, fostering networking opportunities and collaboration among conference participants. 

Moreover, Pontevedra boasts a rich cultural heritage, combining historic charm with modern amenities. Its vibrant streets, plazas, and waterfronts offer excellent venues for conference activities, enhancing the overall experience. Participants can also explore the nearby natural beauty of the Rías Baixas region, which includes stunning coastline, lush landscapes, and renowned vineyards. 

Pontevedra's progressive urban policies, commitment to sustainability, compact layout, cultural appeal, and access to natural surroundings make it an exceptional choice for organizing a conference on degrowth. The city provides an inspiring setting that aligns with the principles and objectives of the conference, fostering meaningful discussions and facilitating a transformative experience for all attendees. 

The activities will take place in several locations. You can find the venue for each activity, as well as other points of interest, by consulting this map.

Pontevedra is located in the province of the same name, in an area known as the "Rías Baixas". This region of the autonomous community of Galicia is famous for its temperate climate, its gastronomic offer, and its beautiful landscapes, both coastal and inland. These are some of the nearby places worth visiting: 

  • Combarro: is a small village a few kilometers from Pontevedra, famous for its large number of hórreos, typical old constructions in Galicia, generally made of stone and raised from the ground on several pillars, which were intended to store and preserve food away from moisture and animals.. 
  • O Morrazo: is a peninsula located between the estuaries of Vigo and Pontevedra where you can find an infinite number of beaches, large and small, familiar and wild. In its westernmost area is Cabo Home, the closest land point to the Cíes Islands.
  • Vigo: this is the biggest town in the region, located only 15 minutes by train from the center of Pontevedra. June is a perfect time to visit this city when the streets are bustling with good weather but tourism is not yet excessive. If you go you should not miss a walk along the port and the old town, and a sunset from a bar on one of its sandy beaches, watching the sun go down behind the Cíes Islands.
  • Atlantic Islands - Cíes, Ons and Cortegada: these terrestrial paradises located in the Atlantic Ocean are small redoubts of marine and terrestrial biodiversity where you will find wonderful beaches, trails, and lighthouses. However, if you wish to visit them, you must take into account that in both Cíes and Ons visits are limited and/or require prior permission to access.
  • Santiago de Compostela: although it is not part of the Rías Baixas, this is a must-see place as it is the capital of Galicia and one of the best-known cities in the autonomous community. It has been a World Heritage Site since 1985, and its cathedral is the destination point for the various pilgrimage routes of the Camino de Santiago (O Camiño, as it is known in Galicia).

These are just some of them, but the offer is enormous. Those fortunate enough to extend their stay will have infinite options for all tastes: sea, inland, beach, mountains, cities, villages, culture, and sports. If you are one of those lucky ones and have doubts, the members of the local organizing team will be happy to share their recommendations with you.
