Special sessions aim to complement the program with relevant topics related to degrowth and ecological economics that are not among those proposed by the organization and have a particular interest to the audience. Each of these sessions will extend for a conference time-slot (with a maximum duration of 1,5 hours), in parallel to regular sessions.

The proposals of special sessions must be submitted using the submission platform before October 16, 2023. To be accepted for evaluation, each proposal must include this information:

  • Title
  • Coordinators: there will be a minimum of 2 coordinators and a maximum of 4 for each special session. Information about organizers will include, for all of them: full name, affiliation, nationality, contact information, a brief explanation of previous experience in the organization of similar sessions, and a short CV.
  • Abstract (up to 1200 words) explaining in general terms what the session consists of.
  • Short summary (up to 150 words)
  • A brief description explaining how the session can contribute to ecological economics and/or degrowth
  • Modality of the session: It must be selected among the 5 modalities indicated above. 
  • A clear description of the dynamic proposed for the session, including a minimum and a maximum number of presenters, the optimum duration, the space requirements, and any other relevant information for the appropriate development of the session (up to 2000 words). This item is especially relevant for interactive and artistic modalities.
  • When possible, a proposal of at least 3 participants who have already confirmed their interest in submitting a contribution to that special session. The information should include full name, affiliation, and contact information.

Any person can submit a special session. Proposals will be evaluated by the organizing scientific committee, with the support of external reviewers, according to the following criteria:

  • Thematic relevance and relation to the central theme of the conference
  • Quality of the proposal
  • Clarity of presentation
  • Originality
  • Interdisciplinarity degree
  • Coordinators experience

Approval of a special session proposal after the evaluation does not guarantee final inclusion in the program. Contributions submitted to special sessions during the “call for proposals” period will undergo rigorous review, similar to that for regular sessions, and must have a minimum number of approved contributions, according to the description indicated in the special session submission.
