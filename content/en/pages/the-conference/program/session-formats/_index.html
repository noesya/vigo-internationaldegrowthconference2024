The Conference will count on two big types of sessions: 

  • Conventional sessions: related to topics proposed by the Organization and the Scientific Committee. The works to be presented within these sessions will be selected by the reviewers designated by the Organizing Committee.
  • Special sessions: selected among the proposals received during the “call for special session proposals” period, that will open in September. Applicants will propose the topics and also the modality of the session, and if their proposals are selected, they will be in charge of organizing their sessions.

The sessions will be developed under different modalities:

  • Parallel sessions
  • Plenaries
  • Interactive sessions
  • Artistic sessions
  • Posters

Parallel sessions will be organized in 1.5 hours slots (having up to 12 parallel sessions per slot). Each session will count on a chair assigned by the Organization and the Scientific Committee, and the presentations will be about a common topic (there can be more than one session about the same topic during the Conference). The topics of the sessions will be those proposed by the Organization and the special topics selected after the “special sessions” call. 

There will be a maximum of 4 presentations per parallel session, and the participants will count on a maximum of 15 minutes for each presentation so that there can be enough time to have an enriching discussion.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - template: chapter title: >- Plenary sessions position: 3 data: layout: no_background text: >-

There will be 2-4 keynote speaker sessions each day. These sessions will have a length of 1 hour, always counting on time enough for the questions and interventions of the attendees. There shall be no parallel sessions during the time slot of the plenary sessions, and there may be a maximum of two plenary sessions at the same time. Some of these sessions will be part of the opening and closing ceremonies.

The invited speakers will be relevant and recognized people in the field of degrowth and ecological economics, who will present their works related to one of the major topics of interest. 

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - template: chapter title: >- Interactive sessions position: 4 data: layout: no_background text: >-

We welcome proposals for interactive sessions that depart from the traditional panel format. These might include book discussions, screenings, performances, walking tours, brainstorming sessions, writing workshops, or whatever else might inspire you.  

Submissions should include an abstract of up to 250 words, as well as an additional paragraph describing the format and any technical, temporal, or spatial requirements (+100 words max). The description should also note whether it will be an open (to submissions) or closed session.

Poster sessions will have the same importance as all the other sessions. Even more, the works accepted under this modality will achieve greater visibility as they will be on public display uninterruptedly for the duration of the event, and a digital version of each poster will be permanently exposed on the website. 

The poster modality will have a one-hour slot during two of the conference days, in which the authors will have the opportunity to explain their work and answer questions from the audience. 

Since creativity is a very important theme for this Conference, the organization board encourages participants to let it fly wild when preparing the posters, without leaving aside the rigorousness but trying to explain the work in a visual way capable of capturing the attention of the public. 

In order to give this modality of participation the importance it deserves, the prize for the best poster (Best Poster Award) will be awarded during the Closing Ceremony. The winner will be chosen by the conference attendees, who will have to rate not only the scientific interest of the work but also its creativity and visual impact.
