Conventional parallel sessions will be organized in 1.5 hours slots (having up to 12 parallel sessions per slot). Each session will count on a chair assigned by the Scientific Committee, and the presentations will be about a common topic (there can be more than one session about the same topic during the Conference). 

There will be a maximum of 6 presentations per parallel session, and the participants will count on a maximum of 12 minutes for each presentation so that there can be enough time to have an enriching discussion.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: heading title: >- Plenary sessions position: 1 level: 2 - kind: block template: chapter title: >- position: 3 data: layout: no_background text: >-

There will be 1-3 keynote speaker sessions each day. These sessions will have a length of 1 hour, each with dedicated time for questions and interventions of the attendees. There will be no parallel sessions during the time slot of these sessions, and there may be a maximum of two at the same time. Some of these activities will be part of the opening and closing ceremonies. 

The invited speakers will be relevant and recognized people in the field of degrowth and ecological economics, and will present works related to one of the conference's major topics of interest.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: heading title: >- Interactive sessions position: 2 level: 2 - kind: block template: chapter title: >- position: 4 data: layout: no_background text: >-

We welcome proposals for interactive sessions that depart from the traditional panel format. These might include book discussions, screenings, performances, walking tours, brainstorming sessions, writing workshops, or whatever else might inspire you. The duration of the interactive sessions should be 1.5 hours, and will be taken during the parallel session slots. 

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: heading title: >- Artistic sessions position: 3 level: 2 - kind: block template: chapter title: >- position: 5 data: layout: no_background text: >-

We welcome practice-based reflections and art works which engage with the goals and messages of degrowth and ecological economics. Creatives including (but not limited to) artists, filmmakers, literary writers, theatre and performance-makers, designers, musicians, street artists, socially-engaged practitioners, collectives, and curators are invited to share and reflect upon their work. Your practice might respond, for example, to subjects such as the personhood of nature, care, hope, interdependence, more than human perspectives, energy, contamination, or waste. We also invite submissions of artworks which will be considered for live and/or virtual exhibition and/or performance during the conference.

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: heading title: >- Posters position: 4 level: 2 - kind: block template: chapter title: >- position: 6 data: layout: no_background text: >-

Poster sessions will have the same importance as all the other sessions. Moreover, the works accepted under this modality will achieve greater visibility as they will be on public display uninterruptedly for the duration of the event, and a digital version of each poster will be permanently displayed on the conference website.

The poster modality will have a slot during several of the conference days, in which the authors will have the opportunity to explain their work and answer questions from the audience.  

Since thinking outside of conventional boundaries is an important theme for this conference, we encourage participants to be creative in preparing the posters, without leaving aside rigor. Aim to explain the work effectively and dynamically to capture public imaginations.
