Imagining post-growth Science, Technology, and Innovation (STI) futures position: 2 data: layout: no_background text: >-
  • Critical examinations of the nexus between economic growth and the technoscience in different contexts and sectors
  • Explore the (uneasy) relations between science, technology and activism
  • Elucidating ways in which post-growth / degrowth can help us rethink STI discourse and practice
  • Exploring the main barriers and opportunities for degrowth-minded ideas to shape our technological and scientific imagination
  • Discussing what levels of technological complexity are compatible with degrowth /postgrowth futures and what are the main trade-offs involved
  • How to challenge platform-based capitalism and the gig economy
  • Post-truths, conspiracy theories, fake news, and the impact of environmental disinformation strategies
  • Discuss STI policies for degrowth 
  • Identify the institutional and governance mechanisms to align STI endeavours with degrowth values concerning democracy, conviviality, autonomy, or simplicity
  • How do we reshape scientific institutions to enable degrowth? 
  • Politics of transition and strategy: how do social movements (eco-socialism and degrowth) can enable transition?
