Special Sessions aim to give space within the Conference to special tracks that are not among those proposed by the organization, sessions with a unique topic and format, or groups of people that want to present the progress or results of a specific project. We invite special sessions that provide artistic or interactive avenues to learn, debate, and co-create in transdisciplinary manners, such as dancing, futuring, visioning, painting, sculpture, songwriting, theatre, improv, poetry, backcasting, world cafés, board games, yoga and meditation etc.

  • Open Special Sessions: sessions organized in the framework of a track related to degrowth and ecological economics that is not among those proposed by the organization and have a particular interest to the audience. This would also be the option for those interested in being in charge of a session with a very specific topic and characteristics within one of the tracks proposed by the organization of the conference. These sessions will be open to the participation of any applicant during the “call for abstracts” period that will open in November. 
  • Closed Special Sessions: organized in the framework of a specific project or activity, always related to the Conference theme. These sessions will not be open to the participation of any applicant, only those allowed by the session proposers and organizers could participate as presenters. 

The selected Special Sessions will be carried out within the parallel session slots. 

Applicants both to Open Special Sessions as well as to Closed Special Sessions must indicate in the application form in which session format their proposed sessions would be developed (conventional, interactive, poster, or artistic modalities).

The Special Session proposals must be submitted through the submission platform before October 16, 2023. To be accepted for evaluation, each proposal must include the next information: 

  • Type of Special Session (Open Special Session / Closed Special Session) 
  • Format of the Special Session: It must be selected among the modalities indicated in the “session formats” page (except the “plenary session” modality) 
  • Title 
  • Organizers: there will be a minimum of 2 and a maximum of 4 organizers for each Special Session. The information provided will include, for all of them: full name, organization, and e-mail. 
  • Track(s): a selection of up to 2 tracks. If a new track is proposed, the submission must include a brief description of it. 
  • Short abstract (up to 150 words) about the proposed session 
  • A clear description (up to 1000 words) of the proposed session and how it could contribute to ecological economics and/or degrowth and the dynamic proposed for the session, including a minimum and a maximum number of participants (presenters), the optimum duration (up to 90 minutes), the space requirements, and any other relevant information for appropriate development of the session. This item is especially relevant for interactive and artistic modalities. For interactive sessions, it will be necessary to include an explanation about what audience participation would consist of.
notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: heading title: >- Evaluation position: 3 level: 2 - kind: block template: chapter title: >- position: 0 data: layout: no_background text: >-

Any person can submit a proposal for organizing an Open or a Closed Special Session. Proposals will be evaluated by the Scientific Committee, with the support of external reviewers, if required, according to the following criteria:  

  • Thematic relevance and relation to the central theme of the conference 
  • Quality of the proposal 
  • Clarity of presentation 
  • Originality 
  • Interdisciplinarity degree 
  • Coordinators experience 

Special Session proposal approval after the evaluation does not guarantee final inclusion in the program. Contributions submitted to special sessions during the “call for proposals” period will undergo rigorous reviewing, similar to that for regular sessions. To be held, the session must count on at least 3 approved contributions or the minimum number indicated in the special session submission. 

notes: >- alt: >- credit: >- - kind: heading title: >- Organization of special sessions position: 4 level: 2 - kind: block template: chapter title: >- position: 0 data: layout: no_background text: >-

Organizers will be responsible for their sessions, always working in close contact with the conference organizers. Some of their functions will be: 

  • Participating in the revision and evaluation of the contributions submitted for their session 
  • Chairing the session or naming a chair 
  • Giving support to the participants of the session and solving their doubts 
  • Ensuring that participants are aware of how the session works and comply with objectives and rules 
  • Any other issue directly related to their session 

In the case of sessions that do not reach the minimum number of approved contributions, or do not cover a full session slot, the conference organizing team may decide whether to cancel the session or merge it with another with a related topic.
