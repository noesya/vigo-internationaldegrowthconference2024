Led by the values of endless growth, monopoly, control, and ‘food from nowhere’ these dominant models of agriculture are a major contributor to environmental degradation and injustices to producers and consumers. Alternative Food Networks (AFN), as situated, relational and care-based ways of imagining and mobilizing agri-food systems, have emerged in response to the challenges posed by these dominant models. This interactive panel brings together 5 women to tell stories of their personal journeys, motivations, experiences and challenges of being a farmer in AFNs in Galicia, Spain.
